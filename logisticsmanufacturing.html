The focus in this bay is on increased efficiency and adaptability in logistics and manufacturing.

The DenseTact project revolves around a small, soft, sensor-equipped gripper. It can recognize and grasp a small screw from a bowl full of assorted hardware.

Also featured here is a mobile robot platform equipped with an advanced robotic arm for quick, autonomous maintenance in data centers, including fiber optic module replacement.

Finally, several of our research groups are interested in how to advance machine learning algorithms and data collection strategies so that humanoid robots can autonomously perform tasks like walking, dancing, handling objects, and interacting with other robots.

Like many of our projects at the SRC, the research showcased here spans multiple research groups at Stanford and involves industrial partners. Our Executive Director Steve Cousins often points out that our robotics faculty know what’s possible and that industry knows what’s necessary. When they collaborate on projects and direction, that leads to maximum impact.
